Volunteering

We are happy to invite volunteers from all around the world to come help at Yensabai Organic Farm. Our sustainable living organic farm is open for sharing ideas. Volunteers are welcome to share their experience and working skills in order to improve our farm and community.  Volunteers will learn and understand more about Lao culture & traditional farming. We offer simple free accommodation and food for volunteers. The food we cook and eat is mostly from our farm and 100% organic. Our working schedule is very flexible, it depends what is necessary to do first, but usually we work in the early morning & later afternoon. We don't have machinery, so most of the work is done by hand tools. At noon we often lie in hammocks, and have time for reading, sipping tea or coffee and talking. Sometimes we have visitors, and volunteers can help with reception and service while sharing information about the farm. Also, volunteers are often invited by the local community to help on their farm, share, learn and join local ceremonies and parties etc.
As we are a small farm and our resources are limited, please take into account the following requirements for volunteering:
- Minimum length of stay: 2 weeks
- Maximum people at one time: 2 persons
- Experience Level: Previous WWOOFing experience and/or knowledge about permaculture is highly appreciated.
• 1-7 nights: 15 USD/person/night
• 7-14 nights: 13 USD/person/night
• 14-30 nights: 10 USD/person/night
If you are interested in volunteering and you are able to stay with us as a volunteer for the required period of time, please contact us via email or facebook at least 1 month before your expected arrival. This will give us the time to respond to you with regard to our availabilities, discuss further details with you, and answer your questions in order to facilitate your stay at the farm.

Please note: If you are interested in staying for a couple of days only, this is of course possible,  but in this case we kindly ask you to opt for a fee-based farmstay.
You can contact us via e-mail to yensabaifarm@gmail.com and via message on our facebook page. We are looking forward to hearing from you!

Our farm is listed on the WWOOF Independents website.

What Our Volunteers Say

“I spent 3 weeks on Yenabai out of my 9 month travels through SE Asia and it was by far the highlight of my trip. Xay has an amazing thing going on his farm. Although it's only a year old, it's almost self sustainable. Connected to the land and disconnected from the cloud. Meals were delicious and made with fresh ingredients from the farm, I learned a lot about cooking traditional Laos style over a fire. Accommodation was either the guest room inside the main honestead, or a tent outside, both of which were lovely. Working days could be anywhere from 8 hours to 2, depending on who comes to visit from the village, but there is always something to do. Xay also implements a strong philosophy of taking things slowly and abandoning traditional city stress and fast paced expectations. We made occasional day trips to Mouang Ngoi for supplies which was really nice as well. I felt fuller, refreshed, a little sore from digging, and ready for the next leg of my journey. Yensabai is a magnificent project and way of living. I cannot recommend more highly.”

Eric

Canada

“I spent three days and four nights with Xay working on Yensabai. For me, it was an idyllic time and I wish I could have stayed longer. The land is beautiful and not only did Xay make me feel super welcome but so did his family and neighbors. I helped out with different projects and chores- building a bridge, feeding the fish, planting corn, okra, and morning glory, harvesting fresh produce and helping to prepare meals. There was always more delicious food than I could eat, and I slept outside in a tent or on a mattress with a mosquito net on the porch. We played music, went to the waterfalls upstream, and spent a lot of time with language exchange. Xay is a super chill, kind, and generous guy. I highly recommend going to work and stay for whatever time you can. I hope to be back again soon.”

Wiley Naman

USA

Think of other options, respect this place.”
